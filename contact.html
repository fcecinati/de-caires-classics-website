What helps us respond well

The more we know about the car before the first conversation, the more useful that conversation will be. If you can, include: the make, model, and year; a brief history of what you know about it; the current condition as you understand it; and what outcome you are hoping for.

Photographs are always useful. Engine bay, underside, bodywork, interior: whatever you have. We do not need professional photographs; honest ones are more useful.

There is no standard quote we can give without seeing the car. But the first conversation will give us both a clearer picture of whether this is the right project for us.
